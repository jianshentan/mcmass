Why would a church want to have a XXX-XXX-XXX?

Churches in modern times suffer from a number of unique problems. In many places across America church attendance is in decline, and churches themselves are even closing down. Churches need to make sure they are financially stable, and need to engage with the larger community around them. A XXX-XXX-XXX franchise represents an opportunity not only for revenue, but also to draw a wider audience to the church, reinforcing the church as a gathering point.

Is there historical precedent for this kind of venture?

Yes! There is a rich history of religious orders using culinary businesses in support of their activities. Perhaps most famous are the Trappist monk breweries, whose beer allows the monasteries to provide for themselves. We want to bring the idea of the self-sustaining church into the 21st century, to help churches prosper and grow.

Is the McMass Project associated with a particular denomination?

No. We at the McMass project believe that innovation is key to the success of all churches, regardless of affiliation, and our top priority is to demonstrate our model so that other churches can follow suit. Our mission is to help the word of God find its way back to the heart of our communities.
7




Who are you?

The McMass project was formed by several members of Lux Dei Design, a Christian design consultancy, who had a vision for the future of churches. We saw so many churches, grand old buildings, fallen into disuse or empty for significant portions of each week. How could we solve this “vacant church problem?” We realized that a design approach--an entrepreneurial approach--had the potential to revolutionize how churches engage with the world. We want to see churches return to the center of everyday life, and the way to engage with our modern culture is through innovation.
